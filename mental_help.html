Mental Health Resources

Are you experiencing thoughts of suicide?

National Suicide Line

The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline provides free support and is open 24hours/7days a week for people who are contemplating suicide. The organization have a confidential support helpline that is also helpful 800-273-8255 and more detailed information on their website suicidepreventionlifeline.org.

Crisis Text Line

The Crisis Text Line is a text hotline that connects an individual to a real-life Crisis Counselor who is trained to handle texters when they experience a crisis. The Crisis Counselor are trained facilitators that use tactics such as active listening skills, problem solving among many others. For more information about the Crisis Text Line, you can visit the website crisistextline.org or text directly by text TALK to 741741

Trevor Lifeline

Veterans Crisis is a hotline dedicated to veterans who served in the military who are determined to stop suicide. The Veterans Crisis Line constantly build new ideas and concepts to support their veterans with initiatives to keep their hopes up such as “Caring Letters”. This program focusses on mailing letters to Veterans during the year after their initial documented call to the Veterans Crisis Line. This initiative has been found to reduce the rate of suicide death, attempts, and ideation and provides a unique opportunity to help save Veteran lives beyond the call. However, you can find more information at www.veterancrisisline.net or dial 800-273-8255 and press 1.

Trans Lifeline

Trevor Lifeline is an organization providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to LGBTQ. This hotline is specifically dedicated to young people under 25. The counselors are trained and understand the challenges LGBTQ faces. The Trevor Lifeline is available 24/7 at 1-866-488-7386 as well by texting “START” to 678678. What makes this hotline unique is you can even email through the computer to speak to counselors
